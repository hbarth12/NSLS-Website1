Our Team

A small team supported by a wider research network.

NSLS brings together student researchers, faculty advisers, policy practitioners, and subject-matter experts to support practical legislative research for Syria's Parliament.

Team Structure

Built around research, review, and practical expertise.

The Network is intentionally lean. Its work depends on coordinated research teams, expert review, and partnerships with people who understand legislative, fiscal, and implementation constraints.

Research Teams

University chapters and student researchers support policy briefs, comparative research, and legislative memos.

Faculty and Expert Advisers

Scholars and subject-matter specialists help review research questions, methods, and analysis.

Practitioner Network

Practitioners in law, public finance, governance, and implementation help keep research grounded in real constraints.

Join the Team

Contribute to nonpartisan legislative research.

NSLS welcomes expressions of interest from students, scholars, practitioners, and institutions interested in supporting evidence-based legislative work in Syria.

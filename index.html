Al-Monitor

How Syria's Wheat Fields Turned Into the First Battleground Over Reconstruction

Syria's wheat sector is becoming an early test of reconstruction policy: who controls procurement, whether farmers can recover, and how public institutions handle a crop tied to food security and rural livelihoods.

The piece looks at why agriculture is not only a technical file, but a political one. Choices over credit, prices, storage, and purchasing can determine whether reconstruction reaches producers outside major cities.

Middle East Institute

Syria's New Investment Law and the Return of State-Mediated Market Access

Syria's new investment framework signals how the postwar economy may be organized. Investment rules determine who receives protection, access, incentives, and opportunities in the sectors most likely to attract capital.

The analysis argues that generous investor benefits can still reproduce unequal access if licensing remains centralized. For Parliament, the core question is how to encourage investment while limiting discretion and elite capture.

Building the research capacity of Syria's Parliament

Syria's 210 MPs will be asked to make decisions across every area of public policy.

Without large professional staffs, parliamentarians need concise, practical resources: facts for debate, analysis for committee work, and independent scrutiny of claims made by government, interest groups, and outside experts.

The Network for Syrian Legislative Studies equips lawmakers with independent research, legislative analysis, and expert knowledge.

A Network of Universities

Building the foundations of an independent parliamentary research service.

Major legislatures rely on large, permanent research staffs. Syria's Parliament does not yet have that infrastructure.

NSLS is building a university-based network to help provide some of the same core functions.

Why This Model

Research Teams

Expert Guidance

An advisory board and growing network of specialists help strengthen the Network's analysis and direction.

Practical Perspective

Practitioners in law, public finance, governance, and implementation help ensure research remains grounded in real policy constraints.
